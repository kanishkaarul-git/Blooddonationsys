Who can donate blood??

1.Are in good general health and free from transmittable diseases.
2.Are aged between 18 and 65 years (some local variations apply).
3.Weigh at least 50 kg (approx. 110 lbs).
4.Have a minimum hemoglobin level of 12.5 g/dL for women and 13.0 g/dL for men.
5.Are not pregnant or breastfeeding.
6.Are free from major chronic illnesses like cancer, heart disease, HIV, or hepatitis B/C.

The Importance

Saves Lives: A single donation can help up to three people.
Constant Need: Blood is needed every day for surgeries, emergencies, and chronic illnesses.
No Substitute: Blood cannot be manufactured; it must come from volunteer donors.
